# The Fischer Indole Synthesis:

## Introduction

The Fischer Indole Synthesis by Emil Fischer stands out as one of the most useful reactions known to chemists. The indole ring (benzene ring fused to pyrrole ring)
so formed is used extensively in medicinal chemistry like the synthesis of Indometacin, Triptan and Vincristine.

## Reaction Mechanism

The Fischer Indole synthesis starts with phenyl hydrazine and a ketone or aldehyde which form phenylhydrazone.

<Image
  src="/images/fischer_indole/step1.png"
  alt="Step 1"
  widthRem="40"
/>

The imine then undergoes tautomerization to form an enamine which is followed by a [3,3] sigmatropic rearrangement and aromatization

<Image
  src="/images/fischer_indole/step2.png"
  alt="Step 2"
  widthRem="40"
/>

The reaction ends with an acid-catalyzed removal of NH3 and an aromatic indole formation.

<Image
  src="/images/fischer_indole/step3.png"
  alt="Step 3"
  widthRem="40"
/>
